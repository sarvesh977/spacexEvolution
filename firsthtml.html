2019

Starhopper Tests (July - August 2019): The "Starhopper" prototype conducted two brief, untethered test flights at SpaceX's Boca Chica facility in Texas, reaching an altitude of approximately 150 meters before being retired in late August 2019

2020 Sn8

December9 Achieved a high-altitude flight to approximately 12.5 kilometers. The prototype performed complex maneuvers but exploded upon landing due to a hard impact

2021 Sn9

February 2 Reached an altitude of about 10 kilometers. Similar to SN8, it failed to land successfully, resulting in an explosion upon impact.

2021 Sn10

March 3 Successfully landed after a 10-kilometer flight but exploded approximately eight minutes post-landing due to a landing anomaly

2021 Sn11

March 30 Experienced an in-flight explosion during descent in heavy fog conditions, resulting in the loss of the vehicle.

2021 SN 15

Successful Landing and Orbital Flight Preparations May 5, 2021: Incorporated numerous upgrades in avionics, software, and engines. Successfully completed a 10-kilometer flight and landing without subsequent explosion, marking a significant milestone in the program..

2023

First Orbital Launch Attempt Integrated Flight Test (April 20, 2023): The first fully integrated Starship and Super Heavy booster launched from Starbase in Texas. Approximately three minutes into the flight, the upper stage failed to separate from the booster, leading to a "rapid unscheduled disassembly." Despite the outcome, the test provided valuable data for future missions

2024

Advancements in Reusability and Booster Recovery Fifth Test Flight (October 13, 2024): Achieved a significant milestone by successfully catching the Super Heavy booster mid-air using the "Mechazilla" mechanical arms upon its return to the launchpad. The Starship upper stage performed a controlled splashdown in the Indian Ocean

2024

Sixth Test Flight (November 19, 2024): The Starship was successfully launched; however, the attempt to catch the Super Heavy booster with "Mechazilla" was aborted, resulting in a controlled splashdown in the Gulf of Mexico. The Starship upper stage completed its mission with a landing in the Indian Ocean.

2024

Seventh Test Flight (January 16, 2025): This test successfully captured the Super Heavy booster using "Mechazilla," demonstrating advancements in rapid reusability. The Starship upper stage was lost during this mission
